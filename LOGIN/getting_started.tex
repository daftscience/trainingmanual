The first time opening \gls{login}, it's a good idea to modify the default values.

\paragraph{Open} \gls{login} by clicking the \appicon{specimen_login} icon from the \gls{ab}.

\paragraph{Select} ``Accession'' from the  ``Log-in By'' options.
\paragraph{Click} \btn{graphics/retrieve.png} to open ``Log-In By ACCESSION.''\\

\section{Setting Location}
The most important setting in \gls{login} \marginnote{\textbf{Fun Fact: }Most \textsc{Log-in} related mistakes
    come from this being set to the wrong location.} is the \textsc{Location}. In most cases, the location will be set to your laboratory's \textsc{Log-in Location}.\\

\noindent
Of course, there are exceptions. Some departments will require an alternate Log-in Location \eg{Microbiology, Blood Bank, \etc}. Departments which require special log-in locations should provide that information.


\importantblock{This setting is ``Sticky'' and there is no ``Default'' value. If you temporarily change it from your normal log-in location,\sidenote[][-1\baselineskip]{\eg{Receiving microbiology samples.}} it needs to be changed back.\sidenote[][1\baselineskip]{This will be a common mistake for people starting out. }}

\paragraph{Select} your \textsc{Log-in Location} from the drop-down menu.\\
