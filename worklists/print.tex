

\section{Print Worklists}

Printing within \textsc{\gls{wlreq}} works differently than many of the other Cerner Applications. \gls{wlreq} uses printers which are defined within Cerner.

\paragraph{Click} the Print Worklist \btn{graphics/print} icon from the tool bar.\\

\prettyimage{width=.5\textwidth}{graphics/print_dialog}

\paragraph{Right-Click} on the \boldcap{Printer List}.

\paragraph{Select} \boldcap{Filter Type}.

\paragraph{Select} \boldcap{Printer}.\\

\prettyimage{width=.8\textwidth}{graphics/right_click_menu}

\clearpage

\paragraph{Select} the nearest printer from the \boldcap{Printer List}.\\

\prettyimage{width=.6\textwidth}{graphics/printer_selected}

\warnblock{This printer selected in this image may not be a usable printer.}

\paragraph{Select} \boldcap{Vertical} for the format.\\

\prettyimage{width=.5\textwidth}{graphics/vertical}

\paragraph{Click} \btn{graphics/ok}


\section{Creating Favorite Printers}
The list of printers to choose from is exceptionally long. However, you may have noticed in the previous section there is a way to set up favorite printers.

\paragraph{Click} the Print Worklist \btn{graphics/print} icon from the tool bar.\\

\prettyimage{width=.5\textwidth}{graphics/print_dialog}

\paragraph{Select} a printer you'd like to add to your favorites List.

\paragraph{Right-Click} on the \textsc{Printer List}.

\paragraph{Select} \textsc{Add To Favorites}.\sidenote{You can select \textsc{Remove from Favorites} to remove a printer.}\\

\prettyimage{width=.6\textwidth}{graphics/add_to_favorites}

\paragraph{Repeat} for all the printer's you'd like to add.

\paragraph{Right-Click} on the \textsc{Printer List}.

\paragraph{Select} \boldcap{Filter Type}.

\paragraph{Select} \boldcap{Favorites}.\\

\prettyimage{width=.6\textwidth}{graphics/favorites}


\newthought{Now, the list} of printers contain only printers you'll use.\\

\prettyimage{width=.5\textwidth}{graphics/small_list}
